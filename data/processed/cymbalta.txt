            <article>
                <h2 id="about_dailymed">About DailyMed</h2>
                <a href="#" class="" id="anch_47">About DailyMed</a>
                <div class="togglecontent">
                    <div>
                        <p>
                            The <a href="//www.nlm.nih.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" id="anch_48">National Library of Medicine</a>
                            (NLM)’s DailyMed searchable database provides the most recent labeling submitted to the
                            <a href="https://www.fda.gov/" target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer" id="anch_49">Food and Drug Administration</a>
                            (FDA) by companies and currently in use (i.e., "in use" labeling).
                            DailyMed contains labeling for prescription and nonprescription drugs for human and
                            animal use, and for additional products such as medical gases, devices, cosmetics,
                            dietary supplements, and medical foods. The NLM provides DailyMed to the public and
                            does not accept advertisements. The labeling on DailyMed is typically reformatted to
                            make them easier to read.
                        </p>
                        <ul>
                            <li><a class="read-link" href="/dailymed/about-dailymed.cfm" id="anch_50"><strong>More Info</strong></a>
